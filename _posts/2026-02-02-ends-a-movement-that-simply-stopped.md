We note the dissolution of a movement that reached no date, suffered no scandal, and made no news — that simply thinned, over a season, until the gatherings stopped being called and no one called them again. Its founder is well. Its members are scattered and, by the accounts we gathered, mostly fine.

We record it because the loud ends get remembered and the quiet ones get the truth. Most movements do not go out in a standoff or a prophecy. They go out the way this one did: a slow return of the ordinary, a Tuesday that no longer needed explaining, a group of people who woke up one morning able, again, to live inside the plain world — and who found, to their surprise, that they could. The desk does not consider this a failure. Some of the time, it is the only real deliverance any of these movements manage, and the ones who reach it are the lucky ones, though they rarely feel lucky at the time.
